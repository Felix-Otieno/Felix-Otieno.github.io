About Our Organization

Our mission

Conserve wildlife and biodiversity in an environment that is uniquely urban, providing a sanctuary for flora and fauna while promoting sustainable tourism and environmental education.

Our Vision

To be a world-renowned urban wildlife park that harmoniously integrates wildlife conservation, tourism, and community engagement, serving as a model for sustainable urban wildlife management.

Our Story

Established in 1946, is Kenya's first national park and uniquely located near Nairobi's city center. It spans 117 square kilometers and hosts a diverse range of wildlife, including lions, giraffes, and endangered black rhinos. The park symbolizes the coexistence of urban development and wildlife conservation, attracting visitors and researchers while involving local communities in its preservation efforts.
